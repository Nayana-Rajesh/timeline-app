1900

Invention of the Radio Transmitter

Around 1900, Guglielmo Marconi successfully developed and demonstrated wireless radio communication, which revolutionized long-distance communication.

1910

First Commercial Radio Broadcast

In 1910, the first commercial radio broadcast took place, marking the beginning of audio broadcasting that reached large audiences.

1920

Birth of Modern Television

The 1920s saw early experiments with television technology, with John Logie Baird and others making significant progress towards electronic video transmission.

1930

Development of FM Radio

In 1933, Edwin Armstrong invented Frequency Modulation (FM) radio, greatly improving sound quality and resistance to interference over AM radio.

1940

Early Electronic Computers

The 1940s witnessed the creation of early electronic digital computers like the ENIAC, which laid the foundation for modern computing.

1977

Introduction of the First Personal Computer

The first personal computer, introduced in 1977, revolutionized the way people interacted with technology.

These early systems were more affordable and paved the way for the digital age and transformed various industries.
